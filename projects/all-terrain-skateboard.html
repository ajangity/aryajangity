All-Terrain Electric Mountainboard

High-torque, all-wheel-drive electric skateboard designed to bridge skateboarding, hiking, and off-road exploration.

Overview & Motivation

As someone who has skateboarded for years while also spending significant time hiking and mountain biking, I wanted to design a platform that combined the agility and freedom of skateboarding with the terrain capability of off-road travel.

Traditional electric skateboards perform well on pavement but fail when faced with loose gravel, dirt, roots, rocks, and elevation changes. This project explores what it takes to build a skateboard that can confidently traverse rough terrain while remaining controllable, efficient, and safe.

Design Challenges

  • Maintaining traction across loose and uneven terrain
  • Traversing large obstacles without rider instability
  • Balancing agility with stability at speed
  • Delivering sufficient torque without overheating
  • Achieving long-range battery life for extended rides
  • Ensuring rider safety during aggressive off-road use

Mechanical Design & Suspension

To address harsh terrain and rider fatigue, I designed a custom suspension system that significantly smooths out impacts from rocks, roots, and uneven surfaces. This allows the board to maintain wheel contact with the ground, improving both comfort and traction.

Larger off-road tires with deep treads were selected to roll over obstacles that would stop traditional skateboards entirely. The increased diameter improves obstacle clearance while the tread pattern provides grip on dirt, gravel, and grass.

Wider trucks were used to increase lateral stability, but they were intentionally tuned looser to preserve a sharp turning radius. This combination allows the board to remain agile while still feeling planted at speed.

Drivetrain, Motors & Traction Control

The board uses a dual-motor, all-wheel-drive configuration, with each motor controlled by its own dedicated VESC. This architecture enables independent torque control at each wheel.

By monitoring wheel speed and motor current, the system can reduce or cut power when excessive slip is detected, effectively acting as traction control. This is critical on loose terrain where uncontrolled wheelspin can lead to loss of control or inefficient power usage.

This all-wheel-drive setup dramatically improves climbing ability, stability under acceleration, and control during technical riding conditions.

Battery System & Range

A high-capacity battery pack was designed and assembled to provide approximately 43 miles of range under typical riding conditions. Cell selection, configuration, and current handling were optimized to support sustained high torque output without excessive voltage sag.

The battery system was designed with serviceability and safety in mind, allowing for inspection, maintenance, and future upgrades.

Rider Safety & Control

Off-road riding introduces the risk of foot slippage, especially when traversing uneven or inclined terrain. To address this, the board includes removable foot straps that can be secured during aggressive riding conditions.

These straps help keep the rider connected to the board over rough terrain while remaining optional. When riding on smoother surfaces such as pavement, packed dirt, or grass, the straps can be removed to restore a traditional skateboard feel.

Results & Takeaways

The final system successfully merges the agility of a skateboard with the terrain capability of off-road vehicles. The suspension, AWD drivetrain, traction control, and battery system work together to create a stable, controllable, and highly capable electric mountainboard.
